Network Programming

In the landscape of modern game development and internet applications,
network programming has become a core skill that every developer must master.
Whether it involves the real-time interactions of multiplayer online games or the data synchronization between clients and servers,
a profound understanding of newtworking principles and practices is indispensable.

In reality, however, many tutorials tend to be fragmented---either focusing solely on theory
or merely providing sample code---making it difficult for learners to truly systematize their newtworking knowledge and apply it within an engineering context.
As someone with extensive experience in game and engine development, 
I deeply appreciate the critical importance of the journey "from theory to practice, and ultimately to hith-performance engineering."

This module was created specifically to address this challenge.
It encompasses a comprehensive curriculum ranging from newtworking fundamentals and TCP/UDP principles to Socket programming and high-concurrency models,
extending all the way to protocol design and game network synchronization.
